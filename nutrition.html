HOW TO LOSE FAT OR GAIN MUSCLES ?? AN INTRODUCTION !!

Ok, so I keep on getting questions like : “I want to lose my bodyweight !! Can you provide me some tips?” OR “Please help me gain weight and pack on muscles. How should I go about them?” I thought this can be a good starting blog to prepare yourselves for the journey. It is difficult to tell you how to lose fat or gain muscle in a short sentence, mainly because multiple factors come into play, especially any medical conditions you might have (more on these in later blogs). But first of all, let me bust some myths here. You, probably like most people, try to measure your progress (losing or gaining) by measuring your bodyweight. However, there is more to it. Traditional eighing scales will tell you the Kgs (or pounds) lost or gained. But does it really tell you what you lost ? Did you lose fat, or in the process, you lost some lean muscle as well? Same with gaining. Did you gain lean muscle, or gained body fat in the process? Ideally, your goal should be to lose fat while keeping your lean muscles as intact as possible. For gaining, you should aim at gaining lean muscles while minimizing the gain in body fat.

OK, AMRITANGSHU !! I GET YOU. BUT HOW ON EARTH DO I GO ABOUT IT?
Well, understand that losing bodyweight and losing body fat are slightly different. Your body is made up of the fat mass and the lean body mass. To reiterate, you should aim at reducing your fat mass, and for gaining, aim at increasing your lean body mass while keeping the other components intact. To do this, first memorize the energy balance equation below:
Net calorie intake = Calories in – Calories out
This means that the amount of calories you are eating vs the amount of calories you are expending becomes important.
To lose fat, calories consumed should be less than calories expended (calorie deficit). For lean muscle gain, calories consumed should be more than calories expended (calorie surplus). PERIOD. AS SIMPLE AS THAT !!

IS IT THAT SIMPLE ?
Let us examine. Your daily energy needs, known as the Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE), is the amount of energy (in calories) that you spend in a day. TDEE consists of 3 components:

  • Resting Metabolic Rate (RMR) : This is the amount of energy (70% of TDEE) you expend while at rest, and is the energy needed to sustain the normal functioning of your body, like blood circulation, temperature regulation and so on. The RMR depends on your age, sex, genetics, food and also, on any medications you are on.

  • Thermic Effect of Food (TEF) : After you ingest your food, your body needs to expend energy to digest and absorb the food to make it usable. This energy (6-10% of TDEE) is called TEF.

  • Exercise and Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis (EAT and NEAT) : This is the amount of energy (20% of TDEE) expended above RMR and TEF during your exercise and other physical activities (for example, during your job). So in short, your daily energy needs depend on how active you are. This is why supplementing a diet regime with the proper exercise regime is so important.

WITH THIS IN MIND, LET US WRAP THIS PART UP WITH FOOD !!
As you may have heard, your diet forms at least 70% of what you achieve. I divide the food you intake into macronutrients, micronutrients and water.
The macronutrients are carbohydrates, proteins and fats. The ratio of these three, often called the macro ratio, is of paramount importance in achieving your goals-- whether to lose fat or gain muscles.
The micronutrients are mainly the vitamins and minerals (like sodium, potassium, calcium, magnesium, zinc and so on). I will cover more on the importance of these in the subsequent articles.
Then comes WATER !! I say, you should literally drink your toxins out !! An average sedentary man or woman should consume on average at least 3 liters and 2.2 liters of water per day. This intake should intake if you are on a journey to lose fat. Some obvious benefits of water:
  • Improves body temperature regulation
  • Improves liver, hormonal and metabolic functions and nutrient distribution throughout the body
  • Maintains blood volume
  • Improves your exercise performance (more on this later)
